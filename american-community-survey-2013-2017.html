3 American Community Survey 2013-2017

The American Community Survey (ACS) is organized by the United States Census Bureau. This survey in in many ways a spiritual successor to the long-form census and contains questions that have been asked systematically by the U.S. government since the 1790 census 2. In their own words3…

The American Community Survey (ACS) is an ongoing survey that provides vital information on a yearly basis about our nation and its people. Information from the survey generates data that help determine how more than $675 billion in federal and state funds are distributed each year.

Through the ACS, we know more about jobs and occupations, educational attainment, veterans, whether people own or rent their homes, and other topics. Public officials, planners, and entrepreneurs use this information to assess the past and plan the future…

—The United States Census Bureau
